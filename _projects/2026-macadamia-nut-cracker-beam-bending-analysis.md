In this assignment we were tasked with analyzing our macadamia nut cracker design in a previous homework, no longer assuming a stiff beam. We had to determine the location of max deflection using beam bending equations and create a beam design such that the maximum deflection of the macadamia nut cracker is less than 2% of its length, and also mass efficient.
Using beam bending analysis, I mathematically validated the conclusion that the location of maximum bending is where the linear slide acts on the nut cracker, the point furthest from the nut. To determine a beam design that is mass efficient, I first conducted a trade study on common materials, looking at the elastic modulus and density of each one to determine the most mass efficient material. I determined that aluminum 2014 was the best for this application, with the highest ratio of elastic modulus to density. With this, I could solve for the minimum moment of inertia I of the beam that would meet the maximum deflection requirement. Since I beams aren't made at such small scales, I chose a circular cross section for ease of grip and sourcing. Using the same beam bending equations, I determined that the radius of the beam would have to be 4.4 mm in order to meet the deflection requirement. This is a great result as it is very reasonable relative to the 80mm length of the nut cracker.
